Projects

Undergrad Research - SPEER

-In the SPEER lab at the University of Maryland, under the guidance of Dr. Colenso Speer and Chenghang Zhang, implemented Computer Vision techniques to classify super-resolution images of neural synapses in the brains of mice. Useful for identifying and visualizing other proteins in these synapses.
-Classified different proteins in synapse images based on physical characteristics using datasets of 3D models of color-dyed synapses.
-Utilized OpenCV and MATLAB to preprocess images and perform image classification using TensorFlow's 3D Convolutional Layers to create a CNN

Undergrad Research - BENSI

-In the RISE lab (Risk Informed Solutions in Engineering Laboratory) at the University of Maryland, under the guidance of Dr. Michelle Bensi and Lingyao Li, used Natural Language Processing techniques to determine loneliness based on the user tweets, tweeting frequency, and social activity.
-Useful for flagging users potentially facing mental health issues. Used Tweepy and TWINT to extract recent tweets from users’ timelines.
-Performed Sentiment Analysis using PyTorch’s BERT model to tokenize the tweet texts and identify key phrases. Learned token extraction techniques and predictive modeling

Capital Canary

-Worked with a team to create a Virtual Reality App that simulates a conversation with your local congressmen to engage in digital advocacy. Users enter a virtual congressional office and are asked questions about a selected campaign and their verbal responses are formatted into an email, which they have the option to send to their real congressmen.
-Used Unity and Android Studio to develop the virtual environment, Node.js and Vue.js to create the website and backend, and Watson Speech to Text to convert verbal responses into text in real-time.
